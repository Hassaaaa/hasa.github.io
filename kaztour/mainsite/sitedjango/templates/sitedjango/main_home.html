About Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan is a transcontinental country located in Central Asia and Eastern Europe. It is bordered by Russia, China, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan, with a coastline along the Caspian Sea. Its capital is Nur-Sultan, and its largest city is Almaty. Kazakhstan is the world's largest landlocked country and has a population of 19 million people. The majority of the population is ethnic Kazakhs, and the country is officially secular but has a Muslim majority.

Almaty, Alma-Ata, formerly (1855–1921) Verny, city, southeastern Kazakhstan. It was formerly the capital of the Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic (1929–91) and of independent Kazakhstan (1991–97). Almaty lies in the northern foothills of the Trans-Ili Alatau at an elevation of 2,300–3,000 feet (700–900 metres), where the Bolshaya and Malaya Almaatinka rivers emerge onto the plain.

Kok Tobe (Kazakh: Көктөбе Kök Töbe - "Blue Hill"; Russian: Кок-Тюбе, Kok-Tyube) is a mountain in Almaty, Kazakhstan's largest city. Kók Tóbe Park, a popular recreation area, is on top of the mountain. The mountain's height is 1100 meters above sea level. Kok Tobe is one of the main landmarks in the city, and it is popular among visitors and tourists to Almaty.

Nomad City or Nomad Fortress is a medieval-themed town located 90 kilometers from Almaty on the left bank of the Ili River. It was built in 2005 for the filming of the movie "Nomads" and gives a unique historical flavor to the picturesque valley of the Ili River, which has been settled by nomads since ancient times. The fortress is so realistic that it can transport visitors to the past.

The Medeu (Kazakh: Медеу, Medeu), is an outdoor speed skating and bandy rink. It is located in a mountain valley (Medeu Valley, or the valley of Malaya Almatinka River) on the south-eastern outskirts of Almaty, Kazakhstan. Medeu sits 1,691 metres above sea level. It consists of 10.5 thousand square meters of ice and utilizes a sophisticated freezing and watering system to ensure the quality of the ice.

Shymbulak (Kazakh: Şymbūlaq, also known is a ski resort near Almaty. It is the largest ski resort in Central Asia. It is located in the upper part of the Medeu Valley in the Zailiisky Alatau mountain range, at an elevation of 2,200 metres (7,200 ft) above sea level. The resort area is about 25 kilometres (16 mi) south of Almaty city by Medeo road. It is popular for its mild climate, large quantity of sunny days and great amount of snow through the winter (from November till May)

Kolsay Lakes National Park is in southeast Kazakhstan on the north slope of the Tian Shan Mountains, close to the border with Kyrgyzstan. The park is known as the "Pearls of Tien Shan" and features the Kolsay Lakes and Lake Kaindy. The park is located between the Raiymbek and Talgar districts of Almaty Region and is about 120 kilometers southeast of Almaty. The lakes were formed in 1887 and 1911 on the Kaindy and Kolsay rivers and are of landslide-tectonic origin.

Shapan is the traditional dress of Kazakhstan and Central Asia, made from thick fabrics such as wool, cotton, primer or double-glazed. It consists of a scarf, a trouser coat, and a jacket jacket. The jacket is sewn like a jeans jacket with a staple made of silver and precious stones instead of a button. Shapan comes in different colors, including two-colored or cropped wool and cotton. The tradition of giving a shapan as a gift to honorable people is widespread among the Kazakh people.

Kazakh cuisine is based on the nomadic lifestyle and features meat dishes like beshbarmak and kuyrdak. Other popular dishes include plov, lagman, and manty. Dairy products such as kumys and shubat are also commonly consumed. Kazakh sweets include baursak and kurt, and tea is the most common drink served with meals.

CHARYN CANYON TOUR

Charyn Canyon (also known as Sharyn Canyon, Kazakh: Шарын шатқалы, Sharyn shatqaly) is a canyon on the Sharyn River in Kazakhstan (200 kilometres (120 mi) east of Almaty, close to the Chinese border). The canyon is roughly 154 kilometres (96 mi) in length.[1] It is part of the Charyn National Park (established on 23 February 2004), and is located within the territory of the Uygur District, Raiymbek District and Enbekshikazakh District (of the Almaty Province). The canyon features peculiar and colourful formations in the likeness of chapels, towers, animals, etc., each formed by the weathering of sedimentary rock. Though it is much smaller than the Grand Canyon, it has been described as being equally impressive
